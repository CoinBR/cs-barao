A Legend

What Made it Legendary?

Today top leagues still use Counter-Strike 1.6 (the most recent version of the original) and international tournaments still host it as one of its most popular titles. CS spread across the globe and found ways to grow in different cultures.

Looking at each version of Counter-Strike we can find that it hasn’t changed so much since the game's inception as a mod for Half-Life back in 2000. Sure, some details in hit boxes, recoil, and aesthetics vary between 1.6, Source, and Global Offensive--but the core, minimalistic gunplay remains intact. That's one of the beautiful aspects of this hardcore, competitive shooter: It hasn't changed much in over a decade because it doesn't need to.

Counter-Strike is brilliant in its Counter-Terrorist against Terrorist execution, as each objective-based match comes are all about teamwork, strategy, and pure skill. There is no helping tricks such as regenerating health. You can’t unlock new weapons with real money. If you die, you stay dead until the next round begins. Even being dead can be intense when you are watching the surviving members of your team rushing to plant the bomb, knowing they're about to get into an ambush. It's usually during death's spectator phase that your teammates discuss a new strategy for the next round.
